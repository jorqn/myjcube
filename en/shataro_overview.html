SHATARO makes formulas learning easier

The SHATARO notation produces formulas which look like real sentences of an imaginary language. Even if they are written with more characters than the ones in classical convention, they represent a smaller number of syllabus, and those syllabus have been chosen in order to form easy to pronounce sentences.

In SHATARO, each syllabus corresponds to a precise cube move. Unlike in standard notation, a syllabus never modifies the meaning of the previous one. For instance, in classical notation, (where each character produce a syllabus) in "R'", the "'" character modifies the meaning of "R" character.

Basic princible

It is quite simple… You only have to add a letter after the letter of the classical notation. "A" for a clockwise move, "O" for a counter clockwise one. For a double move (like "R2"), the "I" letter is used (here "RI").

Therefore we have…

  • «R» (standard notation, 1 syllabus) gives «RA» (1 syllabus)
  • «R'» (2 syllabuses) gives «RO» (1 syllabus)
  • «D2FRF'» (6 syllabuses) gives «DIFARAFO» (4 syllabuses)

Please note that for «U», the used consonant is «T» (like «top»).

  • «U» gives «TA»
  • «U'» give «TO»
  • «URU'» gives «TARATO»
